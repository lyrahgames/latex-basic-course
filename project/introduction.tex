  \section{Introduction}
    We choose the following.
    \[
      x = r \cos \varphi
      \qquad
      y = r \sin \varphi
    \]
    Then we do get
    \[
      r = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}
      \qquad
      \varphi = \arctan{\frac{y}{x}}
    \]
    The result is given by
    \begin{align*}
      \integral{-\infty}{\infty}{e^{-x^2}}{x}
      &= \sqrt{ \integral{0}{\infty}{ \integral{0}{2\pi}{ e^{-r^2} r }{\varphi} }{r} } \\
      &= \sqrt{\pi}
    \end{align*}
